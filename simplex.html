Simplex method solver

This webpage essentially just solves a non-integer linear programming problem specified in matrix form using the simplex method and shows full working.
The problem must be in canonical form, that is: \[ \begin{aligned} &\max{z} = \mathbf{c}^T \mathbf{x} \\ &\mathrm{st.}\\ &A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b} \\ &\mathbf{x} \geq 0 \end{aligned} \] The various matrices can be entered in using Julia/MATLAB/Octave/Scilab notation. In the case of A, semicolons are used to separate rows and commas or spaces to separate individual row elements. \(\mathbf{b}\), \(\mathbf{c}\), \(\mathbf{x}\) and \(x_{\mathbf{B}}\) can have individual elements separated by commas, semicolons or spaces. serialTab is a Boolean that decides whether an additional table is shown at the end, sometimes the final tableau is not solved due to a known bug.
